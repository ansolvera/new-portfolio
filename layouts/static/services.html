Frequently-asked questions

Here are some of the most common questions I get from prospective clients. If you have a question that isn't answered below, feel free to send me an email.

How much will my website cost?

The total cost of your website will depend on the amount of time required to design and develop your project. For example, a content-heavy website like an eCommerce store or a blog will cost more than a simple four-page marketing website. And a full-featured web application will cost more than both. During our introductory phone call, I'll gather detailed information about your project needs, and afterward I'll send over a custom proposal for your review.

Will there be any ongoing costs associated with my website?

Yes, the two most common recurring costs are those associated with web hosting and the CMS. Altogether, these should run no more than $50 per month.

How do I pay?

All payments are processed through Stripe, which accepts Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Apple Pay, and ACH bank transfer. A 50% deposit is required before beginning the project, and the remainder will be invoiced upon completion of the project.

What's the average turnaround time for a website?

On average, I try to shoot for a six- to eight-week turnaround. But the pace of each project is set by the client as well as the complexity of the project. An estimated project timeline is included in each proposal.

Do you also provide website copy?

No, the client is responsible for providing all written content for the website. If you need help, I know a lot of talented copywriters who'll be more than willing to lend you a hand for an additional cost.

Will I be able to manage my own website content?

Yes. Your website will be paired with a powerful, easy-to-use content management system (CMS) like WordPress, Webflow, or Strapi. This ensures that you (or your team members) will be able to easily access and edit all of the content on your site. I'll also record a video training that will teach you how to use your CMS.

Agency XYZ said they can build a website for $500. Why shouldn't I go with them?

You get what you pay for. Steer clear of anyone who tells you that they can build you a website for $500. Chances are, they'll either be using some kind of predesigned template or will outsource the work to an overseas WordPress mill. You won't have any custom design, and your website won't be optimized for SEO, conversions, or performance. In short, it will look like a $500 website.

Can you maintain my website for me?

Sure, no problem! I offer ongoing support in the form of a monthly retainer package. If you're interested, we can talk about this further upon completion of the project.
